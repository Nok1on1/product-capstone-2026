## Project: Problem Statement

**Date:** April 8, 2026

**Validation:** Based on 12 interviews conducted between March 31 and April 8, 2026 (100% confirmation rate).

KIU students who commute via Bus #3 (approximately 400+ students per semester) lose an average of 5 to 12 hours per week to excessive time buffers because the real-time tracking system (tableau and mobile app) consistently provides misleading information — countdowns that reset, freeze, or display buses that never arrive. This forces students to adopt costly coping strategies including extreme early departure (1.5 to 3 hours before class), emergency taxi spend (6–10 GEL per incident), and walking long distances. As a result, students face measurable academic harm (missed quizzes, GPA reduction), financial strain competing with food budgets, sleep disruption, and physical health risks from overcrowded buses.

### Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Primary ICP:** KIU undergraduate students in years 2–4, carrying 5+ courses per semester, who live off-campus in Kutaisi or surrounding areas (Terjola, Samtredia, Khoni) and rely on municipal Bus #3 as their sole or primary commute method to campus.

**ICP Characteristics:**

- Commutes 5+ days per week via Bus #3.

- Has early morning classes (9:00 AM or earlier) at least 3 days per week.

- Limited disposable income (taxi fare competes with food budget).

- No personal vehicle or reliable alternative transportation.

- Intercity commuters (Terjola/Samtredia) are a high-pain subsegment facing multi-stage journeys.
