# Final Problem Statement

**Team:** Bandersnatch
**Date:** April 8, 2026
**Based on:** 12 interviews conducted between March 31 and April 8, 2026

---

## The Problem Statement

> KIU students who commute via Bus #3 (approximately 400+ students per semester) lose an average of 5 to 12 hours per week to excessive time buffers because the real-time tracking system (tableau and mobile app) consistently provides misleading information — countdowns that reset, freeze, or display buses that never arrive. This forces students to adopt costly coping strategies including extreme early departure (1.5 to 3 hours before class), emergency taxi spend (6–10 GEL per incident), and walking long distances — resulting in measurable academic harm (missed quizzes, GPA reduction), financial strain competing with food budgets, sleep disruption, and physical health risks from overcrowded buses.

---

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

**Primary ICP:** KIU undergraduate students in years 2–4, carrying 5+ courses per semester, who live off-campus in Kutaisi or surrounding areas (Terjola, Samtredia, Khoni) and rely on municipal Bus #3 as their sole or primary commute method to campus.

**ICP Characteristics:**
- Commutes 5+ days per week via Bus #3
- Has early morning classes (9:00 AM or earlier) at least 3 days per week
- Limited disposable income (taxi fare competes with food budget)
- No personal vehicle or reliable alternative transportation
- Intercity commuters (Terjola/Samtredia) are a high-pain subsegment facing multi-stage journeys

**Evidence:** "I wake up at like 7:00 AM in Samtredia. I have to catch a marshrutka to get into Kutaisi. By the time I get to the #3 bus stop, it's already 8:00 AM. I just stand there leaning against a wall, hoping the bus shows up so I don't miss my first lecture." (P11 — Tsira B.)

## Why Existing Solutions Are Insufficient

### Current Workarounds Students Use

| Solution | How Students Use It | Why It Fails |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Official tracking app/tableau** | Check countdown before leaving home or at stop | Actively misleading — countdowns reset, freeze, or show buses that never arrive; 10 of 12 students abandoned it |
| **Extreme early departure (1.5–3 hour buffer)** | Leave home 2+ hours before class to guarantee arrival | Consumes 5–12 hours per week; sleep disruption; still fails during full cancellations |
| **Emergency taxi/Maxim** | Call taxi when bus is >20 min late or cancelled | Costs 6–10 GEL per incident; competes with food budget; described as "embarrassing" when borrowing from parents |
| **Walking the full route** | Walk 40+ minutes to campus when bus fails | Arrives exhausted and sweating; dignity cost; impossible in bad weather |
| **Messenger/WhatsApp groups** | Ask peers about bus status in group chats | "Echo chamber of complaints" — no real-time data; anecdotal and unreliable |
| **Checking Instagram/TikTok** | Scroll social media at stop while waiting | Time wasted; no actual information gained |

### Root Cause of Solution Failure

The official tracking tools fail because they were built as **timetable broadcasting systems**, not real-time operational tools. The university and municipal transit operator operate independently with no shared accountability. Students have learned through repeated failure that the system cannot be trusted, forcing them into costly coping behaviors that consume time, money, and wellbeing.

**Evidence:** "I stopped using it after it told me the bus was two minutes away for thirty minutes straight. I just stood there like an idiot. Now I just go early and hope." (P04 — Mariam)

## Component Breakdown

### WHO

KIU undergraduate students who live off-campus in Kutaisi and surrounding areas (Terjola, Samtredia) and rely on municipal Bus #3 as their primary commute method to reach campus, particularly those with early morning classes (9:00 AM or earlier) and limited financial flexibility.

**Evidence:** All 12 interviewees depend on Bus #3 for daily commute. "I am on campus from 9 AM to 5 PM most days. Going home between classes is not realistic." (P03 — Dimitri)

### WHAT

The official tracking tools (tableau displays and mobile app) do not reflect actual bus positions or arrival times. The system shows countdown timers based on scheduled departures rather than live GPS data, causing it to display false confidence — countdowns that reset, freeze at "1 minute" for extended periods, or show buses that have been cancelled without warning.

**Evidence:** "The tableau is a suggestion, not a fact. Last Tuesday, it counted down from 5 minutes to 1 minute, stayed at 1 minute for ten minutes, and then just disappeared from the screen entirely." (P09 — Davit)

### WHEN / WHERE

The problem occurs daily during morning commute hours (7:00 AM to 9:30 AM) and afternoon returns (5:00 PM to 7:00 PM), with severity increasing during exam periods. The issue is concentrated at Bus #3 stops throughout Kutaisi but disproportionately affects intercity commuters from Terjola and Samtredia who face multi-stage journeys with no buffer for missed connections.

**Evidence:** "I wake up at least three hours before my lecture. So if I have it on 9am, I get up at 6am. It is actually very terrible because it is kind of troubling on my sleep schedule." (P03 — Dimitri)

### WHY (Root Cause)

The tracking infrastructure was designed as a timetable broadcasting system for passenger convenience, not as a real-time operational information system. It displays scheduled departure times as if they were live predictions, with no mechanism to communicate cancellations, delays, or loss of GPS signal. The university and municipal transit operator operate independently with no coordination mechanism or shared accountability for student commute outcomes.

**Evidence:** "The digital infrastructure appears to show scheduled departure times rather than actual GPS-based positions. When a bus is cancelled or delayed, the system does not update correctly." (Analysis of P03, P08 responses)

### IMPACT

- **Time lost:** 1.5 to 3 hours of buffer time per day = 7.5 to 15 hours per week per student; across 400 affected students = 3,000 to 6,000 lost student-hours per week
- **Financial cost:** 6–10 GEL per emergency taxi incident; students report choosing between taxi fares and lunch money
- **Academic harm:** Documented GPA reduction, missed quizzes, forced retake negotiations with professors; at least 4–5 quizzes lost per semester by individual students
- **Health impact:** Sleep disruption from 6:00 AM wake-ups; overcrowding causing breathing difficulties and near-fainting episodes
- **Emotional cost:** Chronic anxiety described as "every morning is a gamble," embarrassment from needing parental financial rescue, academic disengagement ("What's the point?")

**Evidence:** "It affected my GPA. I mean, I was late on quizzes, seminars, so scores went down and GPA went down." (P01 — Dachi)
