# 4. Problem Exploration Narrative

This interview showed that the main issue with Bus #3 is not just slow transport, but how unpredictable it is and how unreliable the information is.

Mariam basically plans her life around a two-hour buffer. She wakes up much earlier than necessary just to avoid being late, which costs her sleep and productivity.

One of the most important insights was the “16-minute loop.” The tracking system often resets the arrival time, making it look like the bus is always just 16 minutes away. Because of this, she keeps waiting instead of looking for other options, and ends up wasting a lot of time.

On top of that, her situation is made worse by external factors:

- She cannot afford a taxi
- She has no alternative transport
- She has to wait outside, often in uncomfortable weather

All of this turns a simple delay into stress, exhaustion, and missed classes.

# 5. Mom Test Compliance Check

**Good Example:**  
I asked:  
_"Tell me about a recent time when your commute didn’t go as planned."_

This worked well because it focused on a real experience instead of opinions. It led to a detailed story about her waiting for an hour earlier that day.

**Needs Improvement:**  
I asked:  
_"Does that usually work out well for you?"_ and  
_"What are your most frustrating parts?"_

These were slightly leading because they pushed her toward a negative answer.

**Better approach:**  
_"How does your commute affect the rest of your day?"_

This would let her naturally explain the impact without being influenced.

---

# 6. Personal Reflection

The biggest insight for me was that bad information can be worse than no information.

Before this, I thought the main problem was just waiting too long. But Mariam’s frustration came more from the tracking system giving false hope. The “16-minute” estimate kept resetting, making her believe the bus was close when it actually wasn’t.

If she had known the real wait time, she could have done something useful instead—like staying home or studying somewhere else. Instead, she was stuck waiting in the heat.

This changed how I see the problem. It’s not just a transportation issue—it’s also an information reliability issue. Fixing the accuracy and transparency of bus data could already make a big difference.
