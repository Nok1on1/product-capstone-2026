## Problem Exploration Narrative

- David is an undergraduate student who relies on the unpredictable Bus #3 to commute to campus.

- He independently quantified his friction, calculating that bus delays cost him approximately five hours of wasted time per week.

- The digital tableau at the bus stop displays inaccurate times, functioning as a false reference point that actively frustrates students who plan around it.

- Extreme buffer strategies, such as arriving 90 minutes early, fail completely when a scheduled bus is cancelled or simply does not arrive.

- Students are rational agents actively performing cost-benefit analyses on their transit options.

- Every alternative coping strategy carries a strict penalty in either time, money, or social capital.

- Bus capacity emerged as an unaddressed secondary issue, indicating that reliability also depends on knowing if an arriving bus is too full to board.

- The lack of information extends beyond the city bus, as there is a desire to know if the internal campus shuttle is operating.

## Mom Test Compliance Check

- 

**Followed (Anchoring in the past):** The interview successfully anchored the conversation in actual past behavior by asking about typical commute times. Asking for specifics led David to volunteer a concrete, self-calculated figure of lost time rather than vague, hypothetical complaints.

- 

**Did Not Follow (Ignoring an organic path):** The interviewee brought up bus capacity as an additional pain point without being prompted. The interviewer failed to dig into this specific past experience during the conversation. A more compliant approach would have been to immediately pause and ask David for a specific story about a time he could not board an on-time bus because it was full.

---

## Personal Reflection

- The most striking takeaway was how cleanly David mapped out his own solution space, confirming that no current option is both reliable and free.

- This realization reframed the core problem: the highest-stakes information gap is not bus lateness, but complete bus cancellation, as it removes the student's fallback buffer entirely.

- It was surprising to see that the uncertainty of the system is irreducible with current information, as even arriving an hour and a half early cannot guarantee success.

- The distinct framing of commute costs in terms of time, money, and social capital is highly effective and will be carried into subsequent interviews and the final problem statement.
