## Problem Exploration Narrative

- Dimitri has fundamentally restructured his life and morning routine to accommodate the unreliability of Bus #3.

- The transport issue has escalated from a logistical inconvenience to a physiological harm, forcing Dimitri to wake up three hours before his lecture and causing severe sleep disruption.

- He relies on a layered, actively managed contingency plan that involves arriving an hour early and coordinating potential fallback buses.

- The digital tableau at the bus stop actively compounds the problem by displaying a normal countdown until one minute remains, pausing there for an extended period, and only then declaring a cancellation.

- This specific failure acts as an active misdirection rather than just passive inaccuracy, delaying the student's ability to seek alternative transportation.

- Because notice of cancellation is provided at the last possible moment, students like Dimitri are forced into compounding financial harm by having to order more expensive "express" taxis instead of regular ones.

- The ongoing situation causes chronic stress, making Dimitri highly emotionally expressive and frequently using words like "terrible" and "frustrating".

## Personal Reflection

- The most surprising insight is that the digital countdown running to one minute before displaying "cancelled" is the single most damaging UX failure within the transit system.

- This changed my understanding of the problem space: the tableau is not just lacking information; it provides active misdirection that forces students into stressful, expensive fallback decisions at the last minute.

- It was also sobering to see the extent of "learned helplessness" in the user.

- The fact that Dimitri has fully internalized the problem as a fixed constraint, to the point of accepting sleep disruption rather than expecting the system to improve, reveals just how deep the pain point is.
