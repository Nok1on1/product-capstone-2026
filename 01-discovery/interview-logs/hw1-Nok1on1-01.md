2. ICP Fit Assessment

Fit: Strong. Saba matches the Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) perfectly as a city-based student living in Kutaisi who relies on Bus #3 and #25 for his daily commute. He has experienced direct academic penalties, such as missing quizzes, due to the unreliability of the transit system.
3. Verbatim Quotes

    "I wake up, I watch the time when the bus leaves so I’ll be on time on the bus stop, or I contact my friends... and then I decide if I will take the bus." 

    "Well, sometimes the bus is late and I have to explain myself why I’m late on the quiz at the university..." 

    "I got to the bus station, but the board that usually says when buses arrive was saying that they weren’t working that day..." 

    "Yes, unfortunately I was going to a quiz and I was late, so I had to stay another hour to write the quiz with another group." 

    "I had to ask some locals where I could catch another bus so I could take it to the uni." 

4. Problem Exploration Narrative

The interview revealed that commuting is not a routine for Saba, but a daily tactical calculation. The primary pain point is the "Quiz Tax"—a high-stakes academic penalty where bus delays force him to explain himself to professors or spend extra hours on campus to retake assessments. When digital infrastructure (arrival boards) fails during roadwork, he is forced to rely on "tribal knowledge" from locals to find his way. This uncertainty has led him to favor Bus #25 or private carpooling with friends and parents, as the public utility has lost its reputation for reliability.
5. Mom Test Compliance Check

    Success: I avoided asking "Would you like an app that tracks roadwork?" Instead, I asked about a recent time the system failed. This allowed Saba to describe his actual behavior—asking locals for help—rather than speculating on a hypothetical solution.

    Improvement: I focused heavily on his current frustration with Bus #3. In the future, I should ask more about his "carpooling" coordination. Understanding the specific tools he uses to talk to friends would reveal more about the "competitive" solutions already in place.

6. Personal Reflection

I was surprised by how completely the digital infrastructure fails during route changes. I expected the boards to be slightly off, but the fact they simply say "not working" at the exact moment students need them most is a major insight. This changed my thinking from "improving accuracy" to "managing total system blackouts."
