# Interview Script v1

**Team Name:** Bandersnatch
**Problem this script is for:** KIU students miss important course updates because the LMS does not provide notifications, leading to missed deadlines and repeated manual checking.
**ICP this script targets:** Primary — Third-year KIU Computer Science students who rely on LMS for coursework.
**Version:** v1.0
**Date created:** March 26, 2026
**Created by:** Besik Meskhia, Nikoloz Modebadze, Giorgi Mikaberidze, Nikoloz Kvirikashvili

---

## Script Purpose

This script is a guide, not a rigid questionnaire. Use it to structure the conversation, but follow interesting threads when the interviewee takes you somewhere unexpected. The goal is to learn, not to complete a checklist.

Every question in this script must pass the Mom Test:

- Ask about past behaviour, not future intentions
- Ask about the problem, not your solution
- No leading questions
- No hypotheticals

---

## Screener Questions (Before the Interview)

Before scheduling an interview, confirm the person matches your ICP by asking these 2-3 screening questions over message or email. Do not interview people who do not match.

1. Are you currently a second or third-year student at KIU?
2. Do you regularly use the LMS for your courses?
3. In the past week, have you checked the LMS for updates or assignments?

---

## Interview Introduction (Deliver this, do not read it word-for-word)

"Thanks for taking the time to speak with me. I'm working on a university project where we're trying to understand how students keep track of course updates and assignments. I'm not pitching anything — I just want to understand how things work for you. There are no right or wrong answers. I might take some notes, but everything you tell me is just to help us understand the problem space better. Does that sound okay?

Is it okay if I take notes?"

---

## Phase 1: Opening and Context (5 minutes — 2 questions minimum)

Goal: Understand this person's world and daily routine in the relevant area. Do not mention your problem yet.

**Question 1:**
Walk me through a typical day of classes for you. How do you usually keep track of what you need to do?

**Question 2:**
When you have multiple courses running at the same time, how do you usually stay updated on assignments or announcements?

**Follow-up prompts to have ready:**

- "Can you tell me more about that?"
- "How long have you been doing it that way?"
- "Does that work well for you?"

---

## Phase 2: Problem Exploration (10 minutes — 3 questions minimum)

Goal: Find out whether the problem shows up in their life without prompting it. Let them bring it up if possible.

**Question 3:**
What are the most frustrating or time-consuming parts of keeping up with your coursework?

**Question 4:**
Is there anything you find yourself doing repeatedly just to make sure you’re not missing something?

**Question 5:**
Tell me about a recent time when you almost missed or actually missed something important for a course.

---

## Phase 3: Problem Deep Dive (10 minutes — 3 questions minimum)

Goal: Understand the problem with specificity and depth.

**Question 6:**
Can you walk me through the last time that happened, step by step? From the moment you realized it to how you found out?

**Question 7:**
When that situation happened, what did you do to handle it? Why did you choose that approach?

**Question 8:**
What happened as a result? Did it affect your grade, your schedule, or just your stress levels?

---

## Phase 4: Current Solutions (5 minutes — 2 questions minimum)

Goal: Understand exactly what they are currently using or doing to address this problem, and how satisfied they are.

**Question 9:**
What do you usually do now to make sure you don’t miss updates or deadlines? (e.g., apps, group chats, manual checks)

**Question 10:**
How well does that approach actually work for you? What parts of it still feel broken or annoying?

---

## Phase 5: Close (5 minutes — 1 question minimum)

Goal: Gather any remaining insights, ask for referrals, and leave on good terms.

**Question 11 (catch-all):**
"Is there anything about how you manage course updates that I didn’t ask about but you think is important?"

**Referral question:**
"Do you know anyone else who struggles with keeping track of course updates? Could you connect me with them?"

**Close:**
"This has been really helpful, thank you. If I have a quick follow-up later, would it be okay to message you?"
