**Team Name:** Bandersnatch  
**Problem this script is for:** KIU students relying on Bus #3 face unreliable commute due to lack of real-time tracking, leading to wasted time, stress, and extra transportation costs.  
**ICP this script targets:** Primary — KIU students living off-campus who rely on Bus #3 for daily commute.

## Script Purpose

This script is a guide, not a rigid questionnaire. Use it to structure the conversation, but follow interesting threads when the interviewee takes you somewhere unexpected. The goal is to learn, not to complete a checklist.

Every question in this script must pass the Mom Test:

- Ask about past behaviour, not future intentions
- Ask about the problem, not your solution
- No leading questions
- No hypotheticals

---

## Screener Questions (Before the Interview)

Before scheduling an interview, confirm the person matches your ICP by asking these 2–3 screening questions over message or email. Do not interview people who do not match.

1. Do you live off-campus in Kutaisi?
2. Do you regularly use Bus #3 to get to university?
3. In the past week, have you used Bus #3 in the morning to commute to classes?

---

## Interview Introduction (Deliver this, do not read it word-for-word)

"Thanks for taking the time to speak with me. I'm working on a university project where we're trying to understand how students commute to campus and what challenges they face, especially with Bus #3. I'm not pitching anything — I just want to understand your experience. There are no right or wrong answers. I might take some notes, but everything you tell me is just to help us understand the problem better. Does that sound okay?

Is it okay if I take notes?"

---

## Phase 1: Opening and Context (5 minutes — 2 questions minimum)

Goal: Understand this person's routine and daily context. Do not push the problem yet.

**Question 1:**  
Walk me through your typical morning before coming to university. How do you usually get here?

**Question 2:**  
How does your commute usually work on a normal day?

**Follow-up prompts to have ready:**

- "Can you tell me more about that?"
- "How long have you been doing it that way?"
- "Does that usually work well for you?"

---

## Phase 2: Problem Exploration (10 minutes — 3 questions minimum)

Goal: Let them naturally reveal frustrations without leading them.

**Question 3:**  
What are the most frustrating or unpredictable parts of your commute?

**Question 4:**  
Is there anything you find yourself doing repeatedly just to make sure you get to university on time?

**Question 5:**  
Tell me about a recent time when your commute didn’t go as planned.

---

## Phase 3: Problem Deep Dive (10 minutes — 3 questions minimum)

Goal: Understand the problem in detail and context.

**Question 6:**  
Can you walk me through that situation step by step? From when you arrived at the stop to what happened next?

**Question 7:**  
What did you do when that happened? Why did you choose that option?

**Question 8:**  
What was the result? Did it affect your schedule, your classes, or your stress levels?

---

## Phase 4: Current Solutions (5 minutes — 2 questions minimum)

Goal: Understand current coping strategies and their limitations.

**Question 9:**  
What do you usually do now to deal with bus delays or uncertainty?

**Question 10:**  
How well does that approach actually work for you? What parts still feel frustrating or inefficient?

**Question 11:**  
Do you ever use other buses to get to university? Which ones?

**Question 12:**  
When you use those other buses, do you run into the same kinds of problems Or is the experience different?

---

## Phase 5: Close (5 minutes — 1 question minimum)

Goal: Capture final insights and referrals.

**Question 13 (catch-all):**  
"Is there anything about your commute experience that I didn't ask about but you think is important?"

**Referral question:**  
"Do you know anyone else who relies on Bus #3 and might be willing to share their experience?"

**Close:**  
"This has been really helpful, thank you. If I have a quick follow-up later, would it be okay to message you?"
