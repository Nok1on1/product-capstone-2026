## Key Takeaways

1. **Most common current solution:** Extreme early arrival. Every single participant uses this as their primary strategy. It is universal, fully normalised, and deeply costly — David calculated 5 wasted hours per week unprompted.

2. **Biggest gap:** No solution provides honest, accurate, pre-departure information. The tracking app and tableau are the closest things to a real-time tool, but both are actively misleading — worse than no information because they hold students at the stop on false promise. There is nothing that helps a student decide whether to leave home at all.

3. **Strongest workaround signal:** Nino's Facebook post about the bus got 40 likes — proof of collective awareness and shared pain — with zero official response. The Messenger groups that students already use for informal bus status updates prove demand for real-time peer information, but the format is too noisy to be reliable. Students built an informal system themselves; it just doesn't work well enough.

4. **Critical insight on trust:** Multiple participants have already gone through a full trust decay arc with the existing app — from reliance, through repeated disappointment, to complete abandonment. Any new solution must demonstrate consistent accuracy from day one. Nino stated this directly: "I don't need it to be perfect. I just need it to be honest." She also unpromptedly said she would "pay good money" for a tool that delivered this — the only explicit willingness-to-pay signal in the dataset.

5. **Our opportunity:** A lightweight, honest, pre-departure signal — not necessarily real-time GPS tracking, but reliable status information at the moment of the morning decision. The intercity commuter sub-group (Nikoloz, Tsira) represents the highest-stakes segment: a failed connection costs them the entire morning, not just 30 minutes. A solution that works for them works for everyone.
