## Notes and Observations

- Students who rely fully on Bus #3 are more willing to participate.
- In-person outreach at bus stops is highly effective.
- Messenger and WhatsApp provide good response rates.
- Morning commuters are easiest to reach near bus stops.
- Follow-ups are often necessary to secure responses.
- First-year students show highest frustration and least normalisation.
- Trust in the tracking app follows a decay arc: reliance → disappointment → abandonment.
- Students explicitly value honest information over optimistic predictions.
- Some participants have expressed willingness to pay for accurate real-time data.
- Intercity commuters (Terjola, Samtredia) face a compounded "cascading delay" problem where marshrutka delays feed directly into Bus #3 missed connections.
- For working students, bus delay is a financial loss, not merely an inconvenience — time at the stop is measurable in billable hours.
- Digital signage boards fail silently during route changes (roadwork), leaving students with no official fallback information.
- Academic penalties (missed quizzes, graded sessions) are a recurring and concrete harm — students are forced into bureaucratic recovery processes.
- Peer car-pooling and parental drop-offs are emerging as informal alternatives as trust in public transit erodes.
- The KIU shuttle is perceived as comfortable and reliable but limited to campus territory, creating a persistent "last mile" gap for city and intercity commuters alike.
