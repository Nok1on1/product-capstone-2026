## Scoring Reference

For each filter, score 1 to 3:

- **3** = Clear evidence this passes
- **2** = Possible pass, but uncertain — needs more information
- **1** = Fails this filter

| Filter                | What it measures                                           |
| --------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Real and Recurring    | Happens often enough to justify building a solution        |
| Painful Enough to Act | People are already trying to solve it (workarounds exist)  |
| Underserved Today     | Existing solutions are genuinely inadequate                |
| Accessible to You     | You can interview users this week and build for this space |

## Shortlist

Based on the scores above, our top candidates are:

1. **Missing LMS notifications (general)** — Score: 12/12 — This is a systemic issue with no current solution, affecting the entire student body with high accessibility for building and testing.
2. **Bus #3 unreliable commute** — Score: 11/12 — A daily pain point involving significant time and financial loss for students.

## Team Discussion Notes

The main debate was between LMS-related problems and physical-world problems (transport, food). LMS notification issues stood out due to a perfect score and clear feasibility for a technical solution within our control. While food and housing are high-impact, some members argued they might involve external factors (vendors, real estate) that are harder to influence than a software-based notification layer. We ultimately chose the most widespread and technically solvable problem.

---

## Decision

Our committed problem for Lab 2 is:

**Missing LMS notifications for course updates and announcements**

This problem was chosen because:
It earned a perfect score across all evaluation criteria. It represents a universal frustration for the student body, leads to tangible academic risks (missed deadlines), and offers a clear path for a software solution that we can design and validate directly with our peers.

The runner-up problem we are keeping alive as our secondary ICP candidate is:

**Bus #3 unreliable commute (lack of real-time tracking)**
