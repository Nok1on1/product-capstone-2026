## Candidate ICP 1 (Primary)

**Linked to problem:** Bus #3 unreliable commute (lack of real-time tracking)  
**Submitted by (original problem author):** Besik Meskhia

---

### 1.1 Who Is This Person?

**Segment label:** The Time-Crunched Morning Commuter

**Demographics (relevant to the problem):**

- KIU students living in Kutaisi city center, the "Choma" district, or nearby areas.

* Do not own personal vehicles.
* Fully dependent on public transportation, specifically Route #3.

**Psychographics (relevant to the problem):**

- Punctual, disciplined, and focused on optimizing their daily routine.

* Value sleep and efficient time usage.
* Frustrated by the lack of transparency in public services.
* Tech-savvy and believe that in 2026, waiting blindly for a bus is outdated and avoidable.

---

### 1.2 Their Context

**Where do they experience this problem?**

- At city bus stops, often in poor conditions (rain, cold, or inadequate shelter).

**When does this problem occur?**

- Most critically between 08:00 and 10:00 AM before morning lectures.

**How often?**

- Every weekday, creating a cumulative negative effect.

---

### 1.3 Their Current Workaround

**What do they do today to cope?**

- They use a “safety buffer” strategy, arriving 20–25 minutes early just in case.

* Frequently call taxis or Bolt when the bus fails to arrive within ~10 minutes of the expected time.

**Why is the current workaround inadequate?**

- Financially unsustainable for students over time.

* Wastes up to ~2 hours per week in unnecessary waiting.
* Creates daily stress and uncertainty before classes even begin.

---

### 1.4 Why This Segment

**Why is this a promising target?**  
This problem has a **direct financial, physical, and mental impact**. Unlike digital inconveniences, it affects both time and money. There is also a clear improvement scenario: with real-time tracking, students could wait at home or in a comfortable place until the bus is actually nearby.

**What would need to be true for this to be a strong ICP?**

- Buses must have GPS hardware already installed (used internally but not publicly accessible).
- The current schedule must be inconsistent enough that static timetables are unreliable.

---

### 1.5 Confidence Assessment

**Confidence in this ICP:** High

**What is this confidence based on?**

- Direct first-hand experience from a team member who commutes daily.

* Observed repeated frustration among students at bus stops.
* Informal validation through peer discussions identifying Bus #3 as particularly unreliable.

**What would increase your confidence?**

- Conducting on-site interviews during peak hours (especially in bad weather).

* Asking students to estimate monthly spending on emergency taxi rides.
