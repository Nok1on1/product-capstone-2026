## The Problem Statement

> Third-year Computer Science students at KIU who rely on the LMS as their primary source of course communication experience missing important updates and deadlines when professors upload materials or announcements without any notification system, causing missed assignments, increased stress, and repeated manual checking of the platform. Currently they cope by logging into the LMS multiple times per day, asking classmates for updates, and relying on group chats, which is inadequate because it is inconsistent, time-consuming, and still leads to missed information.

---

## Breakdown

**Who has the problem (specific user segment):**
Third-year Computer Science students at KIU who actively use the LMS for coursework and communication.

**What exactly goes wrong (the pain):**
They miss important updates such as assignments, announcements, and grades, or have to constantly check the platform manually to avoid missing them.

**When and where it happens (context):**
Whenever professors upload materials or announcements to the LMS without notifying students, especially during active academic weeks and high-pressure deadline periods.

**What it costs them (consequence):**
Missed deadlines, lower academic performance, wasted time, and significantly increased stress.

**What they do about it today (current workaround):**
Manually checking the LMS multiple times per day, messaging classmates, relying on unofficial group chats (Messenger, WhatsApp), and asking others if anything was posted.

**Why the workaround is inadequate:**
It is unreliable, depends entirely on other people's diligence, wastes productive time, and still does not guarantee that all updates are seen in a timely manner.
