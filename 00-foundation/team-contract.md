## Decision-Making

**Day-to-day decisions** (does not affect overall direction): any two team members can decide.

**Product direction decisions** (affects what we are building or who we are targeting): simple majority vote, with Program Lead holding the tiebreaker.

**Major pivots** (changing the core problem or abandoning significant work): full team discussion required, minimum 24-hour deliberation period, then majority vote.

**Documentation:** All significant product decisions are documented in a `decisions/` note in the repository with the date, the options considered, and the reasoning for the choice.

## Conflict Resolution

The team agrees to follow the KIU 4-Step Conflict Protocol:

**Step 1 -- Verbal Discussion (15 minutes):** Each party states their position once, clearly and completely. Interrupting is not permitted during this step.

**Step 2 -- Written View (10 minutes):** Each party writes a brief summary of their position and the core reason they hold it. This creates a record and forces clarity.

**Step 3 -- Cool-off (24 hours):** No decision is made immediately after steps 1 and 2. Sleep on it. The written views stay available for both parties to re-read.

**Step 4 -- Program Lead Decision:** If the conflict is unresolved after the cool-off, the Program Lead makes the final call. All parties commit to the decision and document it, including any dissenting view.

**Escalation to instructor:** If the conflict involves the Program Lead as a party, or if the protocol above fails to resolve the issue, either party may contact zeshan.ahmad@kiu.edu.ge to request a mediation session.
